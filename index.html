EMFORCO, the european association for Forced Conscription recognition as war crime and crime against humanity during World War II.

EMFORCO is a collective of historians, experts and citizens acting to recognize forced conscription as a war crime and crime against humanity.
Built on the extensive data points and testimonies from World War II, EMFORCO acts to maintain the memory and improve the education of war crimes and crimes against humanity during WWII involving forced conscripted citizens.
Understanding European unity comes with the recognition of sovereign frontiers and the right of people to choose their flags.
EMFORCO aims to gather decades of archives, testimonials and archives to support the inscription of forced conscription as a crime in European texts, to erect it as a pillar of mutual understanding and sovereignty.

EMFORCO works on 3 key pillars to support its actions :

  • Education : support historical programs with educational materials (archives, testimonials, live presentations).
  • Representation & Public affairs : represent our collective among institutions, administrations and daily ceremonies to support the inscription of forced conscription as a crime in European texts.
  • Mutualization : compile archives, initiatives and expositions to resonate further.
