Our history

EMFORCO was born in 2025 from the will of historians and citizens to put the emphasis on forced conscription, while celebrating 80th anniversaries of WWII end.
Forced conscription has always been a polarizing topic among European people, but the current geopolitical environment forced us to act towards the recognition of forced conscription as one of the highest crimes during WWII.
We are enjoying an era of peace and fraternity that has shown its fragility. EMFORCO aims to reinforce people’s sovereignty and their right to decide their own fate by explaining, educating and advocating against forced conscription in Europe.

Founding Members

  • François Hoehlinger is a French entrepreneur, writer and politician. He graduated from Ecole Nationale d’Administration and wrote several reports and books about diversity in transportation, his foremost domain of expertise. He took over the creation of the EMFORCO collective to tell stories of European forced conscription, including his grandfather Louis.
  • Catherine Hoehlinger is a french specialist of social questions, working for social associations in Brittany. She is supporting the EMFORCO collective to tell stories of European forced conscription, including his grandfather Louis.
  • Nicolas Mengus is a french historian specialized about middle-age and WWII history. He is managing a french newspaper “L’ami Hebdo” and a blog about “Malgrés-Nous”, the history of alsatian and mosellan french forced conscripted soldiers and civilians.
  • Igor Futterer is a french theater regisseur that created WWII focused play “La Cigogne n’a qu’une tête” that has been played for the last 25 years. With Nicolas Mengus, he expanded this project towards a story called Story Roll'Up, an interactive journey with drawings and pictures aiming to explain this story to students.
